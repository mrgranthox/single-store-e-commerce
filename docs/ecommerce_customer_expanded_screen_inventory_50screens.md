# E-Commerce Customer Expanded Screen Inventory (50 Screens)

## 1. Position

For a customer-facing e-commerce system that is meant to match the depth of the 94-screen admin system, the storefront should not be planned as only a catalog plus checkout. It should cover discovery, evaluation, purchase, account management, post-purchase service, trust content, and customer support at a much deeper level.

This revised planning scope sets the customer frontend at:

- **50 screens maximum**
- **40+ serious production screens**
- **50 screens** as the full customer-facing web experience target

This count assumes:

- single-store system now
- future multi-store readiness later
- a full account center
- deeper product and support flows
- dedicated post-purchase service pages
- dedicated trust, policy, and campaign pages

## 3. Why the storefront reaches 50 screens

The screen count grows when the frontend is treated as a full customer experience rather than a basic online shop. The increase comes from splitting major customer journeys into dedicated pages.

### Product depth

A strong product experience includes:

- product detail
- media gallery
- full reviews page
- pre-purchase inquiry or Q&A
- campaign/landing support

### Checkout depth

A serious checkout often separates:

- cart
- checkout shell
- shipping step
- payment step
- review step
- success page
- tracking entry

### Post-purchase depth

A proper account area needs:

- orders list
- order detail
- shipment tracking
- returns
- refunds
- reviews
- support
- security
- preferences

### Trust and support depth

A production storefront also needs:

- support center
- ticket creation
- ticket detail
- complaints flow
- FAQ/help
- policy pages

For this project, the correct planning target is:

- **Customer frontend = 50 screens**

This is the right level of scope for a platform that wants:

- deep admin control
- complete customer lifecycle coverage
